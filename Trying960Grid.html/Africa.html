WHO do we sustain?

Hope Academy School, Kibera slum, Kenya

The school is situated in Kibera slum, on the outskirts of Nairobi city in Kenya. It has been built with the aim of growing the access of slum’s children to education. Because of financial crisis, many families in the slum have no possibilities of school subscription, as education is taxable in Kenya. Hope Academy School targets exactly these children and is continuously aiming to increase the number of children, and consequently the number of teachers and the expansion of spaces.

How is LIFE in Kibera Slum?

The slum supports 1 million persons, 60% of Nairobi’s population on 6% of its surface. The majority of its residents has been migrating here from various parts of the country, in order to find jobs in the popular capital city of Kenya, Nairobi. They regard the slum as a transitory living place until they will get jobs and afford to move in the city. Most of them end up being disillusioned by the lack of work force insertion the city can offer, and end up in having the slum a permanent residence. They take up occasional per-hour paid jobs, that provide them with barely enough support to feed their families.

Because the government does not take the responsibility for the million people living here, there are almost no government educational centers or medical clinical facilities. These are mainly provided and sponsored by international charitable organizations and churches.

Houses are improvisations from mould and plate sheets, with an average surface of 4m squares, where can live up to eight family members.

Only 20% of Kibera has electricity. Until recently, it did not dispose of water, so it had to be collected from the Nairobi dam. This water is not fresh, and often inhabitans develop cholera and typhoid by drinking it. Now, there are two main water pipes and the residents have to pay 3 Ksh per 20 litres.

Because of lack of education and perspectives, children and adolescents are prone to cheap drug usage and prostitution. A high percentage of children are abandoned orphans, suffer from HIV or are physically and emotionally abused.
