Kenya's Next TechKids


The vision behind TechKids

The ambition of this project is to help Kenya's most forgotten kids to become tomorrow's TechKids generation.

Our objective is to encourage these children develop a different set of skills, by offering them access to technology and information and teaching them how to use them.

We believe that by empowering them to handle modern technologies, we create opportunities for their future, which otherwise would be impossible. We believe knowledge will be the trigger for transforming their communities and getting them out of poverty.

First TechKids edition

Kenya’s TechKids starts as a 2 weeks pilot programme, aiming to teach the basics of programming to 20 children.

The children will learn how to use a laptop and will get familiarized with general programming concepts, by using a fun, specially designed learning software for kids. Using this software, they will learn a wide range of things, from creating animations to designing interactive games.

The teaching techniques used are based on intuitive learning, encouraging children to discover concepts on their own, come up with initiative and build the projects they choose. They are meant to stimulate children’s problem solving aptitudes, as well as critical thinking and creativity.

Future perspective

The goal is to extend the 2 weeks programme to a long-term course, where children expand their knowledge, share it with others and become confident on their skills.

For this, we intend to establish long term collaborations with local volunteers from Kenya, who will continue our initiative. We aim to offer them trainings and support in order to help them become self-sustaining.
