Kenya's Next TechKids


The Vision Behind TechKids

The ambition of this project is to help Kenya's most forgotten kids to become a generation of tech kids.

Our objective is to encourage these children develop a different set of skills, by offering them access to technology and information and teaching them how to use them.

We believe that by empowering them to handle modern technologies, we create opportunities for their future, which otherwise would be impossible. We believe knowledge will be the trigger for transforming their communities for the better and getting them out of poverty.

TechKids First Edition

Kenya’s TechKids starts as a 2 weeks pilot programme, aiming to expose children to computational thinking and information technology.

20 children will learn how to use a laptop and will get familiarised with basic programming concepts, by using fun and specially designed learning software for kids. Using this software, they will learn a wide range of things, from creating animations to making music and designing simple interactive games.

The teaching techniques used are based on intuitive learning -- encouraging children to discover concepts on their own, come up with initiative and build the projects they choose. They are meant to stimulate children’s problem solving aptitudes, as well as logical thinking and creativity.

Long-term Vision

The goal is to extend the 2 weeks programme to a long-term course, where children expand their knowledge, share it with others and become confident on their skills.

If the pilot project will prove successful, we intend to follow up by establishing collaborations with local volunteers from Kenya, who will continue the teaching. We plan to offer them trainings and support in order to help them become self-sustaining.

Our Team

We are a group of young people who feel we had the privilege to grow into what we are today by benefiting from access to education and resources. We are eager to give forward this privilege and to share our resources with others.


We have different backgrounds, some are students, IT experts, educators, but what we have in common is our vision to share with others through education.


Some of us have been to Kenya before and were impressed by children there. We admired especially their eagerness to learn, their seriousness in the class, and their appreciation for small things.
