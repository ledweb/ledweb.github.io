Kenya's Next TechKids


The vision behind the project

The ambition of this project is to inspire Kenya's most forgotten kids of today to become tomorrow's generation of TechKids.

Our objective is to help children develop a new mindset and a new set of skills that will turn them into the social transition agents their vulnerable society greatly needs.

The only viable and durable change in their society will come from inside the system, and this requires a better well-equipped, solution-driven individuals, in-line with the global demands.

What is the project about?

We will organize a 2 weeks intensive programming course for 20 children, with ages between 8-15.

Our team of mentors will use adapted learning techniques for teaching them the basics of programming and introducing them to the powerful implications of technology.

The children will get familiarized with general concepts used in programming, by learning how to create they own games and animations.

Learning Methods

The learning techniques used are based on intuitive learning, which will encourage the children to self-discover concepts, come up with their own ideas and build the projects they want to.

The courses are designed to stimulate children's capacity of problem-solving, a critical and strategic thinking, as well as their creativity.

How will the project benefit the children?

The course is meant to develop a certain set of skills for the participants. Besides the familiarization with coding concepts, children will grasp a good sense of lateral skills, which will empower them to later become solution-driven individuals, that will as adults seek the most optimal solutions in solving the long-inflicted problems of their society.

These are skills that are commonly neglected in their education, as a result of lack of strategy and proper materials, as well as poor training and teaching techniques.
